# Miniature Donkey

The **Miniature Donkey** is a breed of ass developed from the donkeys of Sardinia and Sicily. Despite their small size, they are known for their gentle nature and intelligence.

## History

Miniature donkeys were first imported to the United States in 1929 by Robert Green. He brought seven animals from Sicily and Sardinia to establish the breed in America.

## Characteristics

### Physical Features
- Height: 36 inches (91 cm) or less at the withers
- Weight: Typically 200-450 pounds
- Colors: Various shades of gray, brown, black, and spotted
- **Cross marking**: Dark stripe down the back and across the shoulders

### Temperament
- Gentle and affectionate
- Intelligent and easy to train
- Good with children
- Social animals that bond closely with their owners

## Care Requirements

1. **Shelter**: Basic three-sided shelter
2. **Diet**: Grass hay and limited pasture
3. **Water**: Fresh water daily
4. **Companionship**: Should not be kept alone

> **Important Note**: Miniature donkeys are prone to obesity, so their diet must be carefully managed.

## Uses

- Companion animals
- Therapy animals
- Guard animals for sheep and goats
- Driving and showing
