Donkey Breeds

Discover the diverse world of donkey breeds, each with unique characteristics, origins, and purposes. From miniature companions to working animals, learn about these remarkable creatures.

Available breed information

Miniature Mediterranean

Origin: Mediterranean islands

The smallest donkey breed, standing just 26-36 inches tall. Known for their gentle temperament and intelligence, making them excellent companion animals.

  • Height: 26-36 inches
  • Color: Usually grey or brown
  • Temperament: Gentle, intelligent
Learn More about Miniature Mediterranean donkeys Detailed information about Miniature Mediterranean donkey characteristics, care, and history

American Mammoth Jackstock

Origin: United States

The largest donkey breed, developed in America for mule production. These impressive animals can reach over 14 hands high and are known for their strength and endurance.

  • Height: 14+ hands (56+ inches)
  • Color: Black, brown, grey
  • Temperament: Strong, hardworking
Learn More about American Mammoth Jackstock donkeys Comprehensive information about American Mammoth Jackstock donkey breeding, uses, and characteristics

Andalusian

Origin: Andalusia, Spain

A medium-sized breed from southern Spain, prized for their sure-footedness and endurance. Traditional working animals of the Iberian Peninsula.

  • Height: 11-13 hands (44-52 inches)
  • Color: Grey, brown, white
  • Temperament: Hardy, sure-footed
Learn More about Andalusian donkeys Learn about Andalusian donkey heritage, characteristics, and traditional uses in Spain

Poitou

Origin: Poitou region, France

A rare and distinctive breed known for their large size and unique shaggy coat called a "cadanette." Originally bred to produce large mules for agricultural work.

  • Height: 13-14 hands (52-56 inches)
  • Color: Brown with shaggy coat
  • Temperament: Docile, strong
