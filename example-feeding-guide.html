Feeding Your Donkey

Proper nutrition is fundamental to donkey health and wellbeing. Learn about feeding schedules, nutritional requirements, and best practices for keeping your donkey healthy and happy.

⚠️

Important Warning

Never feed donkeys chocolate, avocado, or any moldy feed. These can be toxic and potentially fatal. Always consult a veterinarian before making major dietary changes.

Hay & Forage

Quality forage forms the foundation of a donkey's diet. Understanding different types of hay and forage options is crucial for maintaining optimal health.

Donkeys are efficient at extracting nutrients from poor-quality forage, which can sometimes work against them in modern management. Their digestive system is designed for low-quality, high-fiber vegetation.

Key Information

Daily Hay Requirements
1.5-2% of body weight in dry matter per day (8-12 kg for average 400kg donkey)
Best Hay Types
Barley straw, meadow hay, timothy hay - avoid rich alfalfa
Storage Requirements
Keep hay dry, well-ventilated, and off the ground to prevent mold
Quality Indicators
Sweet smell, green color, free from dust and mold

Concentrates & Supplements

While forage should form the majority of a donkey's diet, some situations may require additional concentrates or supplements.

Most donkeys in good condition with access to quality forage do not require additional concentrates. However, certain circumstances may warrant supplemental feeding.

Key Information

When to Supplement
Pregnancy, lactation, old age, poor body condition, or limited forage quality
Concentrate Amount
Maximum 0.5% of body weight daily (2kg for 400kg donkey)
Suitable Concentrates
Donkey-specific feeds, sugar beet pulp, or low-energy horse feeds

Water Requirements

Clean, fresh water is absolutely essential for donkey health and must be available at all times.

Donkeys can drink 15-30 liters of water per day, depending on their size, the weather, and their diet. Water requirements increase significantly in hot weather or when eating dry feed.

Key Information

Daily Water Intake
15-30 liters per day (more in hot weather or when eating dry feed)
Water Quality
Clean, fresh, free from algae and contamination
Container Requirements
Large enough for the donkey to drink comfortably, cleaned regularly
